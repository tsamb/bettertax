Understanding Capital Gains Tax in New Zealand

A simple guide to how CGT could work for you

What is Capital Gains Tax?

Capital Gains Tax (CGT) is a tax on the profit made from selling certain assets, such as property, shares, or businesses. To help New Zealand transition from having no comprehensive CGT to implementing one, the concept of "V-Day" (Valuation Day) is crucial.

V-Day would mark the starting point for measuring capital gains. In the examples below, we presume V-Day is 1 April 2026â€”the hypothetical date for a New Zealand CGT regime to begin. Any increase in an asset's value before V-Day would not be taxed, while gains after V-Day would be subject to CGT. This approach ensures fairness by only taxing future gains while protecting historical value growth.
